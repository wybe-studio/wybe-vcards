# Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Mixed audience spanning sales teams, corporate departments, small businesses, and freelancers. Users interact with the product in professional contexts — networking events, client meetings, team onboarding. They need to create and share digital business cards quickly, manage team cards centrally, and integrate NFC physical cards. The interface must feel instantly trustworthy and effortless regardless of technical skill level.

### Brand Personality
**Modern, minimal, professional.** The product lets its utility speak — no visual noise, no gimmicks. Every element earns its place. The tone is confident but never loud, polished but never cold. Italian-language UI with an informal "tu" tone keeps it approachable while maintaining professionalism.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone:** Apple / Linear style — ultra-clean layouts, generous whitespace, precise typography, subtle purposeful animations
- **Color philosophy:** Neutral-first (grayscale primary palette) with the brand blue (#008AFF) as the singular accent. Color is used sparingly and intentionally
- **Typography:** Geist Sans with tight letter-spacing on display text. Strong hierarchy through weight and size, not color
- **Theme:** Light and dark mode with system detection. Both modes are first-class citizens
- **Anti-references:** Avoid cluttered dashboards, generic SaaS templates, overly colorful interfaces, or anything that feels "startup-y" rather than established
- **Motion:** Subtle, functional animations only — ease-out transitions, gentle fades. No bouncy or attention-seeking effects

### Design Principles

1. **Clarity over decoration** — Every pixel serves a purpose. If an element doesn't help the user complete their task, remove it.
2. **Consistent restraint** — Use the neutral palette as the foundation. Introduce color only to convey meaning (actions, status, errors). The brand blue (#008AFF) is reserved for the logo and primary identity moments.
3. **Typographic hierarchy** — Guide the eye through weight, size, and spacing rather than borders, backgrounds, or color. Text should do the heavy lifting.
4. **Quiet confidence** — The interface should feel established and trustworthy. Avoid trends, flashy effects, or visual complexity that signals immaturity.
5. **Accessible by default** — WCAG AA compliance across all interactive elements. Proper contrast ratios, keyboard navigation, focus indicators, and screen reader support are non-negotiable.
